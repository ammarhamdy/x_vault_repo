

## Open Standards:
 + Because there are many different manufacturers of network components, they must all use the same standards.
 + In networking, standards are developed by international standards organizations.
 + They also guarantee that the product of no single company can monopolize (احتكر) the market or have an unfair advantage over its competition.
 + Standards organizations are usually vendor-neutral (بائع محايد).

## Standards organizations:
+ Internet Society (ISOC):
	+ Responsible for promoting the open development and evolution if internet.
+ Internet Architecture Board (IAB):
	+ Responsible for the overall managment and development of internet standards.
+ Internet Ingineering Task Force:
	+ Develops and maintains internet and TCP/IP technologies.
	+ Include the process and documents for developing new protocols and updating existing protocols Which are known as request for comment (RFC) documents.
+ Internet Research Task Force (IRTF):
	+ Foucsed on long-term research related to internet and TCP/IP protocols.
+ Internet Corporation (مؤسَّسة) Assigned Names And Numbers (ICANN):
	+ Based in the United states, ICANN coordinates(إحداثيات) Ip address allocation.
+ Internet Assigned Numbers Authority (IANA):
	+ Responsible for overseeing (الإشراف) and manageing IP address allocation, domain name management, and protocol identifiers (معرفات) for ICANN.    


## Electronic and Communications Standards:
+ Other standards organizations have responsibilities for promoting and creating the electronic and communication standards used to deliver the IP packets as electronic signals over a wired or wireless medium.


## Communications Standards organizations:
